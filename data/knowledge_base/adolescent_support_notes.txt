Confidence at school:
Confidence grows through practice, preparation, and support. A student can build confidence by setting small goals, preparing for class, asking questions, joining positive peer groups, and speaking to a trusted teacher or mentor.

Exam stress:
Exam stress is common. Helpful steps include making a revision timetable, taking breaks, sleeping well, eating regular meals, and asking for help early. If stress feels too heavy, a student should speak with a trusted adult, school counselor, parent, guardian, or qualified professional.

Online safety:
Young people can stay safer online by keeping passwords private, avoiding sharing personal information with strangers, blocking or reporting harmful messages, and speaking to a trusted adult if something online feels uncomfortable or unsafe.

Career planning:
Career planning can begin with identifying interests, strengths, school subjects one enjoys, and skills one wants to develop. Students can speak with teachers, mentors, family members, or career guidance officers to learn about different pathways.

Learning digital skills:
Digital skills can help young people access education and future work opportunities. Beginner skills include typing, using spreadsheets, searching safely online, creating documents, and learning basic programming or data analysis.

Support seeking:
Asking for help is a sign of strength. A young person can speak with a trusted adult, teacher, counselor, parent, guardian, community worker, or local support service when facing difficult situations.
